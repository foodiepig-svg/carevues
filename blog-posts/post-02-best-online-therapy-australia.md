# Best Online Therapy Platforms in Australia (2025 Ranking)

## Why Australians Are Turning to Online Therapy

The Australian mental health system has a waitlist problem. Bulk-billed psychology sessions are increasingly hard to find. Private in-person therapy costs $150-280 per session — often out of reach for the people who need it most.

Online therapy platforms have stepped into that gap. But which ones actually work in Australia? Which have real Australian therapists? Which are worth your money?

We tested and reviewed the major platforms. Here's what we found.

### 2. Talkspace — Best Value for Text-Based Therapy

**Rating:** 4.3/5

Talkspace is a solid alternative to BetterHelp, with a reputation for responsive therapists and a slightly lower price point. Australian therapists are available. The platform has improved significantly over the past 2 years.

**Pros:**
- Slightly cheaper than BetterHelp
- Good therapist responsiveness
- Flexible plans

**Cons:**

- 30-day cookie vs BetterHelp's 90-day

**Starting from:** ~$49 AUD/week

### 3. Reclaim — Australian-Owned

**Rating:** 4.1/5

Reclaim is one of the few Australian-owned and operated online therapy platforms. It was founded by Australians who wanted a service designed specifically for the Australian context — including Medicare and NDIS pathways.

**Pros:**
- Australian-specific
- Medicare & NDIS pathways available
- Local support team

**Cons:**
- Smaller therapist network
- No affiliate program (so we can't link to it)

**Starting from:** ~$89 AUD/session (package deals available)

### 4. Mental Health Online — Best Free Option

**Rating:** 3.8/5

Mental Health Online is a not-for-profit providing low-cost and free mental health support to Australians. It's not a full replacement for therapy — it's more of a guided self-help program with professional oversight.

**Pros:**
- Free/low-cost
- Australian-based
- After-hours support

**Cons:**
- Not 1:1 therapy — more structured programs
- Limited slots

**Cost:** Free to low-cost

---

### 5. OntheLine — Best for Youth

**Rating:** 4.0/5

OntheLine is specifically designed for young Australians aged 15-25. It offers free text-based counselling, making it ideal for those who find phone calls daunting.

**Pros:**
- Free for under 25s
- Text-based (no pressure to talk)
- Youth-focused

**Cons:**
- Age-restricted
- Not full therapy

**Cost:** Free

## What to Look for in an Online Therapy Platform

Before choosing, consider:

1. **Your budget** — BetterHelp starts at ~$60/week. That's $240/month — cheaper than 2 in-person sessions but not negligible.
2. **Your communication preference** — Do you prefer text? Video? A mix?
3. **Urgency** — Most platforms match you within 24-72 hours. None are for crises.
4. **Therapist switching** — BetterHelp lets you switch freely. This matters more than you'd think — the first match isn't always right.
